# Sales Performance Analysis Dashboard

---

## Project Overview

This project analyzes and visualizes the **sales performance of Amaefule Store** using transactional sales data. The goal is to **transform raw sales records into actionable insights** that help answer:

> *“How is our sales performing, and where should management focus attention?”*

The project features an **interactive sales dashboard** with **summary tables and KPIs** providing a **clear, executive-level view** of sales trends, product performance, city contributions, and sales representative effectiveness.

## Dataset and Metrics Understanding

Understanding the dataset is a critical step in ensuring accurate insights. The dataset includes:

* Customers
* Sales transactions
* Products
* Sales representatives
* Cities
* Monthly sales data

Key performance indicators (KPIs) derived from the dataset:

| KPI                 | Value          | Business Meaning                 |
| ------------------- | -------------- | -------------------------------- |
| Total Customers     | 700            | Active customer base size        |
| Total Revenue       | $472,800       | Overall sales performance        |
| Average Sales Value | 575            | Typical transaction size         |
| Total Quantity Sold | 1,700 units    | Sales volume across products     |

These KPIs allow stakeholders to quickly understand performance at a glance.

## Sales Performance by Time (Monthly Analysis)

### Revenue Trends by Month

* Highest revenue month: **November ($47,400)**
* Strong months: July, October, January
* Lowest revenue month: **April ($22,200)**

Insights:

* Mid-year slowdown in April
* Year-end performance spike due to promotions or seasonal demand

Actions:

* Plan targeted promotions in low-performing months
* Ensure adequate inventory and staffing before peak months

### Quantity Sold by Month

* Highest quantities: **June and July (177 units each)**
* Lowest quantity: **April (82 units)**

Observation:

> Revenue depends on **volume, product mix, and pricing**. High quantity does not always equal high revenue.

## Dashboard Design and Visualization

Design focused on **clarity, storytelling, and usability**:

* KPIs displayed prominently
* Line charts for trends
* Bar charts for comparisons
* Donut charts for percentage contributions
* Soft color palette to reduce visual fatigue
* Filters for year and performance categories

Benefits:

* Non-technical users can understand insights instantly
* Analysts can explore patterns in depth
